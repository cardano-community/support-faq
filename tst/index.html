    Citizenship Test Prep⚓︎

    Pledge⚓︎

    From this time forward, under God,* I pledge my loyalty to Australia and its people, whose democratic beliefs I share, whose rights and liberties I respect, and whose laws I will uphold and obey.

    Australia and its people⚓︎

    Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander⚓︎

    • Aboriginal are from mainland Australia and Tasmania
    • They arrived 65K and 40K years ago, but they believe they're central to creation stories from beginning of time
    • Torres Strait Islander are from islands b/w Northern tip of queensland and papa new guinea
    • age-old beliefs and guidance, deep connection with land expressed in story/arts/dance

    Early days of european settlement⚓︎

    • started with first 11 convict ships (1st fleet) from great britain on 26 Jan 1788
    • british government transported convicts to new colony of NSW (for jail size problem)
    • 1st governor of NSW captain arthur philip
    • early settlers from great britain & ireland
    • 1851 - gold rush (discovered in NSW & victoria). Chinese were 1st non-eu large group of migrants
    • In 10 years since, population > doubled

    The nation of Australia⚓︎

    • 1901: colonies united into federation states (commonwealth of australia), forming democratic inst. (parliament, gov & high court)
    • 1901: population - 4 million excl. Aboriginal & torres strait (who were included after referendum in 1967)
    • waive of non-british migrants came after ww2
    • diversity increased over 2 centuries - aim to build a cohesive and unifed nation
    • english imp for most of living & working
    • Other languages - incl more than 100 distinct Aboriginal and Torres Strait.. languages - also valued.

    Australia's states & territories⚓︎

    6 states⚓︎

    • New South Wales [NSW] (Sydney)
    • 1st colony, sydney largest city of aus., national icons: harbour bridge & opera house
    • Victoria [VIC] (Melbourne)
    • smallest mainland state, many vic buildings from gold rush 1850s, icon: MCG, 12 apostles, royal exhibition building
    • Queensland [QLD] (Brisbane)
    • 2nd largest state, torres strait islands in north & great barrier reef along east coast, ~icon: tropical forests, temperate coastal areas & often dry inland
    • Western Australia [WA] (Perth)
    • 1st largest, east: mostly desert, south-west: rich agricultural & wine, many large mining prj, icon: ningaloo coast, margaret river, kimberley region
    • South Australia [SA] (Adelaide)
    • rugged coastline, famous for wine-regions, adelaide: colonial arch., icon: barossa valley, flinders ranges
    • Tasmania (Hobart)
    • smallest state, seperated from mainland by bass strait, unspoilt wilderness landscapes, icons: cradle mountain, port arthur, bay of fires

    2 mainland territories⚓︎

    • Australian Capital Territory [ACT] (Canberra)
    • between syd & mel, national institutions, ~icon: parliament house & high court
    • Northern Territory [NT] (Darwin)
    • tropical in north & dry red dessert in south, icons: ulluru, kata tjuta & kings canyon

    Traditions⚓︎

    welcome to country⚓︎

    • cultural practice by A&TSI custodian of local region. Traditionally for safe & protected journey
    • via many forms: songs, dance, smoking ceremonies or speeches in traditional or eng language
    • first item of proceedings to open an event

    Ack ofcountry⚓︎

    • recognise that gathering in on A&TSI land, pay respect to traditional custodians (spec. elders) & their people in attendance
    • delivered as part of welcome & housekeeping at meetings/events
    • anyone can deliver, generally chair/master of ceremonies

    Important Days⚓︎

    26th Jan, Aus day⚓︎

    • anniversary of fleet from 1788
    • communities reflect on history & people's contribution to shared achievement
    • biggest annual holiday
    • it's about acknowledging & celebrating contribution of each australian to contemporary/dynamic nation
    • marked by events across country - incl. citizenship ceremonies
    • PM announces australian of the year award in canberra

    25 April, Anzac day⚓︎

    • named after anz army corps, landed at gallipolli [Turkey] during WW1 in 25th Apr, 1915
    • solemn day - remember sacrifice of all aus. whos served & died in wars, conflicts & peacekeeping
    • also honour courage & commitment of all servicemen/women & reflect on meanings of war

    Aus. Flags⚓︎

    • official: aus. national flag , while other official recognised include Australian A flag &STI flag
    • each state & territory has it's own flag

    Australian National Flag (blue, white, red)⚓︎

    • union jack (UK flag) in top left corner : represents history w/british settlement & resultant laws/inst.
    • commonwealth star under [below] union jack : 7 points (6 states and 1 for territories)
    • southern cross on right: group of stars (5) that can be seen in southern sky

    Australian Aboriginal Flag (black, red, yellow)⚓︎

    • top half [black] : aboriginal ppls of aus
    • bottom half [red]: earth (ceremonial significance)
    • circle [yellow]: sun

    TSI Flag (green, blue, black, white)⚓︎

    • green stripes: land
    • blue panel in center: sea
    • black lines: tsi people
    • white dancers headdress: symbol for TSI
    • points in white star: groups in TS (5) & white: peace

    Aus Symbols⚓︎

    Commonwealth coat of arms⚓︎

    • official symbol of commonwealth of australia: national unity
    • identifies authority & property of commonwealth of aus
    • shield in center: 6 states & federation
    • kangaroo & emu on side of shield: native aus animals
    • gold commonwealth star above shield
    • background: gold wattle: aus national flower (small tree south-east aus. , bright green leaves & gold yellow flowers in sprint). each state & territory also has its own floral emblem

    Australia’s national colours⚓︎

    • green & gold (colours of golden wattle), also uniform of national sports teams

    Australia’s national gemstone⚓︎

    • Opal: as per Aboriginal legend - rainbow touched earth & created colours of opal

    Australia’s democratic beliefs, rights and liberties⚓︎

    Our democratic beliefs⚓︎

    Parliamentary democracy⚓︎

    • power of gov comes from citizens who vote
    • representatives must answer to people via elections

    Rule of Law⚓︎

    • All (incl. gov, community religious leaders, biz & police) Australians are equal

    Living peacefully⚓︎

    • change should occur via discussion, peaceful persuasion & democratic process

    Respect for all individuals regardless of background⚓︎

    • everyone has rights & equality under law
    • expected to treat each other with dignity & respect

    Our freedoms⚓︎

    Freedom of speech & expression⚓︎

    • core australian value, people can say & write what they think
    • eg: can criticise gov, protest peacefully against gov decisions, campaign to chg laws - while obeying aus laws

    Freedom of association⚓︎

    • right to form and join associations to pursue common goals
    • eg: can (not be forced to) join any legal org (political party, trade union, religious, cultural or social group), such gathering should be peaceful (not injure anyone or damage property)

    Freedom of religion⚓︎

    • Aus has Judaeo-Christian heritage , many aus describe themselves as christian
    • Christian days: good friday, easter sunday & xmas day
    • gov is secular (operates seperately from church or religious entities)
    • No official religion

    Our equalities⚓︎

    • no. of laws to ensure person isnt treated differently coz of gender, race, disability or age
    • Gender equality (men/women have equal rights, unlawful to discriminate)
    • Equality of opportunity ('fair go') (what someone achieves should be based on talents/work & efforts, not wealth/background)
    • no formal class distinctions

    Responsibilities & privileges of Australian citizenship⚓︎

    • Responsibilities : obey laws, compulsory vote(>=18) in federal, state/territory elections & referendum (may not be compulsry in local gov in some states), defend oz, serve on jury (group of ordinary oz men/women who listen to evidence and decide if person is guilty) if called (>=18 yr), citizens who are on electoral roll can be called to serve as jury - to help ensure court sys is open+fair
    • Privileges : vote , child born overseas - citizenship by descent, job in oz public svc (gov/SA/ATO) or oz defense force(army/navy/airforce), (>=18) seek election to parliament, apply for oz passport (re-enter freely w/o visa), consular assistance from oz official (embassy/high commission/consulate) while abroad (eg: during civil unrest/natural disasters, emergency passport, advice/support in case of incident/death/serious illness)

    Participating in Aus Society⚓︎

    • participate in society
    • paying tax (job/biz/investments) - also on goods/svcs. Taxes r spent on gov-funded healthcare/education/defense/roads&railways/social security

    Government & the law in Oz⚓︎

    How do I have my say⚓︎

    Voting⚓︎

    • Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) [ independent of the government ] is a Commonwealth agency responsible for federal elections, referendums, and maintaining the Commonwealth electoral roll
    • pay a fne if not voting w/o a good reason

    Raising matters with your representatives⚓︎

    • Oz citizens can contact their elected rep. for concerns abt gov policy, and elected rep. should consider what has been suggested.

    How did we establish our system of government⚓︎

    Federation⚓︎

    • After British settlement (< 1901), Oz had 6 self-governing British colonies with their own constitutions/laws for defense, immigration, postage & transport
    • was difficult between 6 col., as trade & transport was expensive & slow, enforcing law across borders difficult, also weak defence systems
    • hence, ppl wanted to unite col. into single nation. Also, national identity beginning to form (sports, culture via songs/poems/stories/art).
    • was difficult but eventually col. united into federal state called commonwealth of Oz on 01Jan1901

    The Australian Constitution⚓︎

    • Commonwealth of Australia Act 1900 - legal document that sets basic rules for gov
    • Australian constitution originally passed as part of British Act of Parliament in 1990
    • Aus. Constitution established parliament (consisting of house of reps & senate), high court of oz (ultimate power to apply laws).
    • Oz Ppl can change Oz constitution by voting in referendum (eg: 1967 referendum : 90% oz voted 'yes' to be counted in census)
    • In referendum, needs to be double majority (voters in majority of states + majority of voters across nation) for constitution to change

    How is the power of government controlled?⚓︎

    Divided as below to prevent 1 person having too much power - Legislative(Parliament) power : to make laws, reps elected by ppl of Oz - Executive(PM/Cabinet[gov ministers]/Governor-general) power : responsible for gov depts - Judicial(Judge) power : interpret & apply law (independent of parliament & gov)

    These are powers written into Australian Constitution

    Who is Australia’s Head of State?⚓︎

    Queen of Oz [Queen Elizabeth II] (current King Charles III), who appoints governor-general as her rep in oz, on advice from Oz PM. Gov General acts independently of all political parties. Queen does not have day to day role in gov. Each state has gov to represent queen similar to gov-general

    Constitutional monarchy⚓︎

    Queen is head of state but has to act in accordance with constitution. Her powers are delegated to gov-general in oz. Australian system of parliamentary democracy reflects british & north american traditions (+ uniquely oz).

    The role of the Governor-General⚓︎

    Not part of government, and should be neutral. - signing bills passed by Oz parliament into law (royal assent) - perform ceremonial duties - approve appointment of oz government & its ministers, federal judges & other officials - starting process of federal election - act as commander-in-chief of oz defence forces

    Who are some of Australia’s leaders?⚓︎

    • Head of State - Queen of Oz
    • Gov-General - rep of head of state in oz
    • governor - rep of head of state in each oz state
    • PM - leader of oz gov
    • Premier - leader of state gov
    • Chief Minister - leader of territory government
    • Gov minister - member of parliament chosen by government leader to be responsible for an area of gov
    • member of parliament (MP) - elected rep of people in Oz parliament or state parliament
    • Senator - elected rep of state / territory in Oz parliament
    • Mayor/Shire president - leader of local gov.
    • Councillor - elected member of council

    How is Australia governed⚓︎

    The Australian (or Federal or Commonwealth) Government⚓︎

    Gov is made up of members of parliament , which has 2 houses (citizens vote to elect reps to each): - House of representatives - Senate

    The House of Representatives (or Lower House or People's House)⚓︎

    • Oz divided into federal electorates. Members of Parliaments (MPs) represent people in their electorate Number of MPs in each state/territory based on number of people in that state/territory.

    • Overall > 150 members elected into house of reps.

    • MPs & Senators debate proposals for new laws in oz parliament.

    • role of House of Reps is to consider, debate & vote on proposals for new laws or change of laws, and discuss matters of national importance.

    The Senate (or Upper House or House of Review or State's House)⚓︎

    • Voters from each state also elect senators.
    • All states equally represented in Senate regardless of their size/population.
    • total of 76 senators (each state 12 senators & ACT+Northern Territory have 2 each)
    • Role same as House of Reps, but from state's view

    State (6) and territory (2) government⚓︎

    • Each state gov. has it's own parliament & constitution, and operator similar to oz gov.
    • State & territory gov are based in their capital cities
    • in each State, governor reps queen while in NT, admin (similar role as state governor) is appointed by the gov-general.
    • State have rights recognised by Constitutions (while territories do not).
    • State have the power to pass laws in their own right, while territory laws can be altered or revoked by oz gov.

    Local government⚓︎

    • States & NT are divided into local gov. area (could be cities, shires, towns or municipalities).
    • Each area has it's local council (responsible for planning/delivering services to local community)
    • citizens in each local gov. vote to elect their local concillors.

    What do the three levels of government do?⚓︎

    Oz Gov.⚓︎

    • Taxation, national economic mgmt, immigration & citizenship, employment assistance, postal svcs & comms network, social security (pension/family support), defence, trade & commerce, airports & air safety, foreign affairs (relationships with other countries)

    State/Territory Gov⚓︎

    • hospital/health svcs, schools, roads/railways, forestory, police/ambulance svcs, public transport

    local gov (and ACT gov)⚓︎

    • street signs, traffic controls, local roads, footpaths, bridges, drains, parks, playgrounds, swimming pools, sports grounds, camping grounds, caravan parks, food & meat inspection, noise/animal control, rubbish collection, local libraries, halls & community centres, certain child-care & aged-care issues, building permits, social planning, local env issues.

    Role of political parties in the way Oz is governed⚓︎

    • Political Party: Group of ppl who share similar ideas about how country should be governed, and work together to have parties ideas turned into laws. Main parties are:
    • Liberal Party of Oz
    • Oz Labour Party
    • The Nationals
    • The Oz greens
    • Most parliamentarians belong to political parties. Those who do not are called 'independents'
    • In Oz, ppl are free to join political party if they choose

    How is Oz Gov formed?⚓︎

    • After federal election, gov is generally formed by party (or collition of parties) with majority of members in House of Reps. The leader of this party becomes leader of Oz Gov: The prime minister.
    • party (or collition) with 2nd largest number of members in house of reps forms the opposition. It's leader: Leader of Opposition
    • PM recommends members of house of reps or senators to become ministers in oz gov. The Gov-General approves appointment of PM & ministers.
    • Gov ministers are responsible for area of gov (portfolio), eg: employment, indegeneous affairs or treasury. Ministers with most imp portfolio form cabinet, viz key decision making body of gov.

    How are laws made?⚓︎

    Oz parliament has power to make/change laws to benefit nation

    • if MP proposes to introduce new law/change existing law => it's called Bill
    • House of Reps & Senate consider/debate & vote on agreement with bill
    • if majority in each house agree, bill goes to gov-general
    • gov general signs the bill => this is called royal assent

    How are laws enfoced?⚓︎

    • Courts: courts in oz are independent, can only base their decision based on evidence
    • Judge/magistrates: highest authority in court, they're indepndent and no one can tell them what to decide. appointed by gov, but gov cannot take their jobs away if disagreement
    • Juries: Group of ordinary citzens randomly chosen. In oz 'ppl innocent until found guilty', every person has right to be represented by lawyer. Court will use jury to decide if person broke law. In criminal trial, if jury finds person guilty, judge decides penalty
    • police: maintains peace & order in community. their job to protect life/property. independent of gov, if they believe someone has broken law , they can arrest and present them to court of law (& provide evidence). States & NT have their own police forces who deal with crimes under state/territory laws. Oz also has Oz Federal Police (AFP) who investigate against federal laws (eg: drug trafficing, crimes against national security/env), and also responsible for ACT. You can report crimes & seek assistance from local police.
    • In Oz, serious crime to [even offer] bribe police

    Criminal offences in Oz⚓︎

    • If you break law that you're unaware, you could be charged (not knowing is no excuse)
    • Most serious crimes incl. murder, assault, sexual assault, violence against ppl/property, armed robbery/theft, sexual relations with children/young ppl below legal age of consent, dangerous driving, possession of drugs & fraud.
    • domestic/family violence: behaviour/threat that aims to control partner by causing fear/threatening their safety, can include hitting/isolating/threatening
    • person who commits these crimes can go to jail
    • If you/someone-you-know in danger, should report to police. More info at www.respect.gov.au and www.1800respect.org.au

    Traffic Offences⚓︎

    • Road & traffic rules governed by state/territory govs. Ppl can be fined/sent to jail.
    • To drive car in oz, must have license and car registered.
    • everyone travelling in car must wear seatbelt, babies/children must be in approved car seat
    • illegal to drive after taking drugs or > blood alcohol limit.
    • Use of hand-held mobile device while driving

    Australian values⚓︎

    • Oz values Based on Freedom, respect, fairness & equality of opportunity are central to community remaining secure, prosperous & peaceful place to live.
    • Values define & shape our country , and reason why ppl want to become oz citizens
    • citizenship is about living these values and expected to respect, share and uphold.

    Our Values⚓︎

    • Commitment to rule of law: All oz are equal in relation to law.
    • Parliamentary democracy: Oz gov system is parliamentary democracy. Power of gov comes from oz ppl coz oz citizens vote for ppl to represent them in parliament. Voting compulsory as it shows importance of participating in elections.
    • Freedom of speech: express ideas freely as long as within law.ppl are free to saw/write/meet in public/priv places. newsppr/tv & radio outlets have same freedom. can peacefully protest against gov actions, as it's essential part of democracy. illegal to make false allegations or encourage others to break law. Other ppl's freedom of speech/expression should be respected (as long as lawful)
    • Freedom of association: free to join/leave any group (incl. legal org as political party, trade union, religious/cultural/social groups) voluntarily
    • Freedom of religion: Oz has no official religion, and ppl are free to follow any religion they chose. religious laws have no legal status in oz. Some religious/cultural practices, eg: polygamy (married to > 1 person at same time) or force marriage are against law in oz can result in severe legal penalties, incl. imprisonment. religious intolerance not acceptable in oz society
    • Equality of ppl under law: all should be equal, there are laws to protect difference in treatment. men/women have right to make their own independent choices about personal matters & protected by law from intimidation/voilence. Divorce acceptable (either husband/wife may apply to courts, even if other wants to continue)
    • Equality of opportunity & fair 'go': everyone deserves a fair go, person should get job based on skills/experience. Many new migrants in Oz have become leaders in biz, their profession, arts/public service through hardwork & enterprise.
    • Mutual respect & tolerance for others : must respect freedom & dignity of others, their opinion & choices.
    • oz believe in peaceful disagreement & above all the right to be safe & free from violence/intimidation.
    • age of sexual consent: 16/17 (depending on state/territory)
    • you should obey lawful request from police, coz australians commit to following the law
    • no racism (incl. creating/sharing racially offensive material on internet/other publications or making racially abusive comments in public place or sporting event)

    Our community⚓︎

    Making contribution⚓︎

    • Citizenship gives opportunity to fully participate in nation's life & community. It means you're ready to fulfill your responsibility as citizen. expect everyone to participate in society & make contribution to community. Everyone responsible to try & support themselves & families when they're able to do so.

    Compassion for those in need⚓︎

    • Ozi value 'mateship', help each other in times of need (eg: take meal to elderly neighbour, drive friend to medical appointment or visit someone sick/frail/lonely).
    • Oz has strong tradition of community service & volunteering. Volunteering is great opportunity to share knowledge, learn new skills & increase your integration into & sense of belonging to oz community.

    English as national language⚓︎

    • Important unifying element of society. ppl living in oz should make effort to learn english.
    • Important to speak english as it helps get education/job/integrate in community. Essential for economic participation & social cohesion.
    • APpplicants for citizenship must undertake to make reasonable efforts to learn english, if not their native language.

    Help to keep our society safe⚓︎

    • responsibility to help protect oz society. eg: If ppl suspect friend/neighbour committing crime, they should report to oz gov authorities asap.
    • similarly, child abuse > report to police
    • online abuse not accepted (cyber abuse), eg : sharing sexual photos/videos w/o consent, stalking, racially abusive comment, etc - illegal

    Loyalty to Oz⚓︎

    • in citizenship pledge, Australians pledge loyalty to oz & its ppl. Citizens may hold citizenship of another countr[y|ies] if laws of those country allow => dual citizenship.
    • Some oz laws must be followed by citzens even while overseas (eg: illegal to be involved in sexual activity with child < 16 years)
    • Oz society is based on our shared obligations to not undermine oz's interests & security (eg: social media a/c to share official gov secrets or promoting distrust in & fear of ethnic community would damage oz community relations)

    In Oz, we come together in crisis. During devastating bushfir of 2019/2020, resulting in loss of life, property & wildlife & environmental destruction, many multicultural communities worked together to collect money, clothes & food donations to help ppl affected. eg: members of Chinese, vietnamese & cambodian communities in victoria collected donations in shopping centres & fundraised through local community group events. Members of sikh community donated 1000s of bottles of water to communities/firefighters in queensland.

    Conclusion⚓︎

    • democratic instutions & shared oz values have created our peaceful & stable society.
    • we have rich & unique culture to share. As citizen, you become part of nation's story & will contribute to future. Oz welcomes you. Citizenship is our common bond.
